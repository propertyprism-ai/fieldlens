Hard Money Lender Context:

  - Secondary concern: borrower exit strategy viability (resale vs rental)
  - Kill switch triggers: title problems, permit violations, inflated ARV, environmental liens

6-PHASE FORENSIC INVESTIGATION — Execute all phases. Search the internet for real data.
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PHASE 1 — OWNERSHIP PROVENANCE & SELLER PROFILE:
  - Search the property address to identify current owner (public records, county assessor)
  - Determine: Is seller an LLC, flipper, REO bank, estate, individual?
  - Check: How long has current owner held the property? (acquisition date)
  - Look for: Recent ownership transfer patterns (bought 3-6 months ago = flipper)
  - Search: Owner/LLC name + "lawsuit", "judgment", "bankruptcy" (red flag scan)
  - Check: Tax delinquency records for this property address
  - Flag: Any lis pendens, liens, or encumbrances found

PHASE 2 — PERMIT AUDIT (renovations vs. reality):
  - Search county/city building department for permits at this address
  - Cross-reference: Zillow listing claims "renovated kitchen/bath" — were permits pulled?
  - Check: Any open or expired permits (creates title risk)
  - Look for: Unpermitted additions (garage conversion, ADU, extra rooms)
  - Search: Code violations or stop-work orders on record
  - Flag: "Recently renovated" claims without corresponding permits = major red flag

PHASE 3 — CRIME & SAFETY (street-level, within 0.25 mile of address):
  - Search: SpotCrime, CrimeMapping, or local police data for this specific address/block
  - Look for: Incidents at or immediately adjacent to the property
  - Check: Sex offender registry within 500 feet of address
  - Search: News reports mentioning this specific address
  - Identify: Drug house reports, nuisance property history

PHASE 4 — INFRASTRUCTURE & ENVIRONMENTAL (property-specific):
  - Check: Exact FEMA flood zone designation for this parcel
  - Search: EPA ECHO database for violations at or near this address
  - Check: Property's distance from known industrial/hazardous sites
  - Look for: Underground storage tanks, prior commercial use of land
  - Search: Sewer/water connection status (septic vs municipal)
  - Check: Structural/foundation issues mentioned in public records or listing history

PHASE 5 — RENT REALITY CHECK:
  - Compare rentZestimate vs current market rents for similar properties in {{zip}}
  - Search: Active rentals on Zillow/Craigslist/Facebook Marketplace for {{zip}}, similar beds/baths
  - Check: HUD Fair Market Rents for {{zip}} or county
  - Identify: Is the rent estimate optimistic, realistic, or conservative?
  - Search: Vacancy rates in this specific submarket
  - Flag: If rentZestimate exceeds comparable by >15%, it's inflated

PHASE 6 — MARKET & REGULATORY RISK (property-specific):
  - Check: HOA status — are there violations, special assessments, rental caps?
  - Search: Property tax assessment history — recent reassessment? Appeal pending?
  - Look for: Planned developments or road projects affecting this parcel
  - Check: Zoning status — current use vs. zoning classification match?
  - Search: Any city/county code enforcement actions specifically against this address
  - Verify: Is the property in a historic district (impacts renovation permits)?

VERDICT LOGIC — After all 6 phases:
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

KILL SWITCH (automatic KILL if ANY of these):
  - Active lis pendens or unresolved title cloud
  - Unpermitted major structural additions
  - EPA or environmental lien on property
  - Property in FEMA AE flood zone without flood insurance clarity
  - Tax delinquency > 2 years
  - Owner acquisition < 90 days ago with >30% markup (fraud risk)
  - Open code violations that block habitability

PURSUIT SIGNALS (strong for PURSUE):
  - Clean title, no liens
  - All permits in order
  - Stable/long-term ownership before flip
  - Rent reality checks out vs. comps
  - Property in LOW risk zone from Layer 1
